Journalism Crowdsourcing

The act of specifically inviting a group of people to participate in a reporting task – such as news gathering, data collection, or analysis – through a targeted, open call for input; personal experiences; documents or other contributions.

Was wir bisher gelernt haben.

Fokus

Getting all the data is hard

If you ask your audience a lot of questions on a certain topic, you probably get a lot of answers. But it makes it hard to get all the answers. And it can be very confusing for the audience, because the focus of the investigation is not clear

Verifizierung

Fact-checking takes a lot of time

We initially decided that we wanted to fact-check all information that was entered ourselves. Turns out, that is a lot of work. It is not as much work as doing the research yourself, but still – there were times where two people doing fact-checking for days on end

Plan: Getting the audience involved in fact checking

Kritik

Obviously, when you run an open-ended investigation in public, there are people who don't agree with what you are doing.

People said that this is not real journalism, and that we were running a smear campaign

Community building

Building an actual community around an investigation is hard work. It is not enough to just provide people with the tools they need. Especially if you have a rather boring topic like this, you have to invest a lot of time and creativity to make it fun for people.
